## Abstract

The Theory of Learning by Exception (TAE) reformulates learning as a process driven by structural discontinuities rather than statistical accumulation. When applied to Artificial General Intelligence (AGI), this paradigm challenges conventional digital architectures and demands physical substrates capable of sustaining irreversible local transformations while preserving global coherence. This whitepaper develops a unified framework integrating TAE with neuromorphic processing units (NPUs) based on memristive devices, under the METFI model, which conceptualizes the Earth as an internally forced toroidal electromagnetic system. We propose that neuromorphic substrates are not merely energy-efficient accelerators but physically suitable hosts for exception-driven learning, exhibiting measurable electromagnetic correlates analogous to biological cognition. Experimental programs, metrics, and reproducible validation protocols are presented without reliance on regulatory or conflicted sources, grounding the analysis in fundamental physics, neuroscience, and complex systems theory.

**Keywords:** TAE, AGI, METFI, neuromorphic computing, memristors, toroidal fields, learning by exception, electromagnetic coherence.

## 1. Conceptual Shift: From Accumulation to Exception

Learning systems based on gradient descent and probabilistic inference assume continuity, reversibility, and convergence. TAE rejects these assumptions. In TAE, learning occurs when a system encounters an event that cannot be projected onto its existing representational manifold. The exception is not noise; it is the primary informational driver.

In physical terms, this corresponds to a localized loss of symmetry followed by structural reintegration. Such processes are ubiquitous in biological cognition, geophysical dynamics, and phase transitions. They are conspicuously absent from conventional digital AI architectures.

> [!NOTE]
> TAE defines learning as **irreversible local transformation with reversible global coherence**.

## 2. METFI as a Physical Boundary Condition

The METFI framework models the Earth as a toroidal electromagnetic system with internal forcing. Within this view, complex systems embedded in the terrestrial environment are weakly but non-trivially coupled to global electromagnetic boundary conditions.

This does not imply deterministic control. Instead, it introduces phase, coherence, and resonance constraints that shape the dynamics of embedded systems. For AGI architectures with physical substrates, METFI provides a macroscopic field context analogous to environmental constraints in biological evolution.

## 3. Neuromorphic Processing Units and Memristive Matter

Memristors exhibit state-dependent conductance governed by their full current history. Memory and computation are inseparable. Each learning event alters the physical state of the device.

Unlike digital weights stored in external memory, memristive states are:

* Continuous rather than discrete
* History-dependent
* Non-identical after reset

This makes NPUs inherently compatible with TAE. An exception induces a permanent microphysical change, while network-level coherence can be preserved.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Memristive drift is not a flaw. It is the physical substrate of learning by exception.
