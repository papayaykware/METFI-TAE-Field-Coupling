## **Abstract** <a id="abstract"></a>

This whitepaper establishes a rigorous framework to evaluate **resilience** and **coherence** in Artificial General Intelligence systems designed under the **Theory of Learning by Exception (TAE)** and physically contextualized within **METFI**, where Earth is modeled as an internally forced toroidal electromagnetic system. Extreme geomagnetic, ionospheric, and geophysical events are treated not as exogenous noise but as **structurally informative signals** capable of inducing non-linear phase transitions in coupled cognitive systems. Resilience is redefined as irreversible structural reconfiguration with preserved functional identity, while coherence is formalized as an **asynchronous, field-dependent variable**. The document integrates non-linear physics, neuroelectromagnetic analogies, and distributed AGI architectures, and proposes **programs of seguimiento** that leverage natural events as excitation functions without reliance on conflicted institutional sources.

Within **METFI**, Earth is an active electromagnetic system whose internal forcing modulates learning environments at scale.
