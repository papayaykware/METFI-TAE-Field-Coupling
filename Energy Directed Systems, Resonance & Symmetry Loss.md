## 1. Introduction

> [!NOTE]
> **Key shift:** From energy accumulation â†’ structural compatibility.

Directed energy has traditionally been framed through power density, flux and damage thresholds. While suitable for linear systems, this paradigm fails when confronted with complex, far-from-equilibrium structures. In such systems, small perturbations can precipitate macroscopic reorganizations.

The common denominator is not energy magnitude, but *phase relationship*. Resonance enables internal energy redistribution, allowing systems to reorganize without significant external input. This principle is observable across geophysical, biological and cognitive domains.

## 2. Resonance as an Organizing Principle

Resonance in complex systems is not a point condition but a **dynamic regime**. It involves progressive phase alignment, mode redistribution and coherence emergence across scales.

A resonant system does not respond because it receives more energy; it responds because it stops dissipating incoherently. Energy already present becomes ordered.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Resonance acts as an *active filter*, not a passive amplifier.

## 3. Phase Synchronization & Weak Coupling

Weakly coupled oscillatory systems synchronize phase without losing structural autonomy. Mathematical models (Kuramoto-type networks) show that synchronization depends more on *topological compatibility* than frequency identity.

Directed energy effectiveness therefore lies in **channel access**, not dominance. Once coupling occurs, the receiving system performs the reorganization.
