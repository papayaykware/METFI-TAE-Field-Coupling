## Abstract

The **Theory of Learning by Exception (TAE)** introduces a structural shift in artificial learning systems by redefining the exception as the primary carrier of information, rather than statistical regularity. This whitepaper articulates TAE within the **METFI framework** (Toroidal Electromagnetic Model of Internal Forcing), proposing a class of **asynchronous, toroidally coherent AGI architectures** based on hybrid NPUâ€“memristor substrates.

The work focuses on scalability toward distributed networks, addressing temporal desynchronization not as a defect but as an informative gradient enabling ontological reconfiguration. Neurobiological analogies, non-linear electromagnetic coherence, and topological memory are integrated into a unified model, accompanied by concrete **follow-up programs** designed to track phase stability, hysteresis, and semantic emergence.
