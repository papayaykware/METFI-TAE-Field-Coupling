## Abstract

The Theory of Learning by Exception (TAE) proposes a non-statistical learning paradigm centered on the detection, persistence, and structural integration of non-expected events within complex systems. Recent work has demonstrated that TAE-aware AGI architectures can maintain coherence without global synchrony and exhibit robustness under variable noise conditions. In parallel, the METFI model conceptualizes the Earth as an internally forced toroidal electromagnetic system, where symmetry loss induces non-linear geophysical and biological effects.

This whitepaper integrates both frameworks to address a critical unresolved challenge: the experimental validation of TAEâ€“AGI in environments characterized by real-time, unpredictable noise, such as large-scale IoT networks and open cyber-physical systems. We develop a rigorous methodological extension grounded in non-equilibrium physics, propose formal metrics aligned with exception dynamics, and outline follow-up experimental programs consistent with the METFI framework.

---

## 1. Introduction

Learning in contemporary artificial systems is largely predicated on statistical regularity. Even robust or adaptive learning approaches assume that noise is, in principle, modellable. TAE introduces a structural rupture in this assumption by treating persistent exception as a primary informational carrier rather than a residual error.

In METFI, the Earth itself is modeled as a toroidal electromagnetic oscillator whose internal symmetry losses manifest as coherent, non-linear events rather than random perturbations. The conceptual convergence between TAE and METFI lies in their shared emphasis on learning and evolution through structured instability.

---

## 2. Prior Experimental Validation

Initial validations of TAE-aware architectures have been conducted in controlled and semi-open environments using memristive substrates, neuromorphic processing units (NPUs), and asynchronous distributed systems. These experiments demonstrated:

* Coherence without global synchronization
* Robustness under variable noise amplitude
* Persistence of exception as a measurable state variable

While significant, these environments remain near equilibrium and therefore insufficient to probe the full dynamics of exception-driven learning.

---

## 3. Real-Time Unpredictable Noise

In large-scale IoT and cyber-physical systems, noise exhibits properties fundamentally distinct from classical stochastic disturbances:

* Strong non-stationarity
* Hidden cross-scale correlations
* Semantic interference
* Structural feedback loops

Such noise is co-generated by the system itself, mirroring the internal-forcing paradigm of METFI.
