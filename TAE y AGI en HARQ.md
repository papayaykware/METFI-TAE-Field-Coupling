## Abstract

Artificial General Intelligence research has largely relied on homogeneous architectures optimized for continuous statistical learning. This assumption fails under conditions of semantic rupture, regime shifts, and non-linear discontinuities. In parallel, DARPA's HARQ program introduces a radically different paradigm in quantum computing: functional integration of physically heterogeneous technologies within a single coherent system.

This whitepaper formalizes the **Theory of Learning by Exception (TAE)** as a foundational framework for AGI, proposing that true learning emerges through topological reconfiguration triggered by exception, rather than incremental optimization. Drawing from heterogeneous computing, neurobiological electromagnetic fields, bioinformatic genetics, and planetary-scale models such as METFI, the paper articulates AGIâ€“TAE as a coherent, physically grounded and scalable architecture.

## 2. Theory of Learning by Exception (TAE)

TAE posits that learning occurs when a system encounters an internal contradiction that cannot be locally resolved. Instead of gradient correction, the system performs **topological reconfiguration**.

Key properties:

* Non-continuous learning
* Episodic restructuring
* Energy-intensive transitions
