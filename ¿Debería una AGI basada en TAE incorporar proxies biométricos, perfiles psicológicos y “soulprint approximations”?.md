## Abstract

The **Theory of Learning by Exception (TAE)** proposes a radical departure from dominant predictive and optimization-based paradigms in artificial intelligence. Rather than learning through statistical regularities, TAE formalizes learning as the detection of structural ruptures, symmetry loss, and phase transitions in complex systems. This whitepaper develops a conceptual architecture for an **Artificial General Intelligence grounded in TAE (AGI-TAE)**, integrating biometric proxies, dynamic psychological profiles, and *soulprint approximations* as constitutive dimensions of the cognitive state space.

The framework is articulated in dialogue with the **METFI model**, which conceptualizes Earthâ€”and by extension living and cognitive systemsâ€”as electromagnetically toroidal systems driven by internal forcing. Within this view, non-linear effects emerge from symmetry loss rather than external shocks. We argue that AGI-TAE must operate as a system of **coherence tracking**, not prediction or control, enabling learning through collapse events. Finally, we propose concrete tracking programs to explore these hypotheses across neurobiological, cognitive, and systemic domains.
