## Abstract

The Theory of Learning by Exception (TAE) introduces a paradigm in which relevant information does not emerge from statistical regularity, but from the persistence and internal coherence of anomalies within noisy, non-stationary environments. In parallel, the METFI model conceptualises the Earth as an internally forced toroidal electromagnetic system, whose geomagnetic variability induces non-linear effects across geophysical and biological systems.

This whitepaper articulates a rigorous integration between TAE and METFI applied to distributed AGI architectures. Geomagnetic variations are treated not as exogenous noise, but as structured informational fields capable of inducing adaptive topological reorganisation. The framework enables functional coherence without global synchrony and is supported by physics, complex systems theory and electromagnetic neurobiology. Programmes of seguimiento are proposed to enable empirical validation at scale.
