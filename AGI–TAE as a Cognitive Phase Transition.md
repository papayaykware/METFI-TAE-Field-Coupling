## Abstract

The pursuit of Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) has historically relied on continuous, statistical, and accumulative learning paradigms. While effective in stable environments, these approaches exhibit structural fragility when exposed to discontinuities, regime shifts, or low-probability high-impact events. This whitepaper introduces the **Theory of Learning by Exception (TAE)** as a necessary cognitive phase transition enabling AGI systems to reorganize their internal representational topology in response to structural incompatibilities. AGIâ€“TAE is framed as a prerequisite architecture for robust Artificial Superintelligence (ASI), drawing deep analogies with non-linear physical systems, symmetry breaking, and phase transitions described in METFI and ECDO models. The work focuses on theoretical foundations, architectural implications, and concrete programs of seguimiento, avoiding speculative futurism and regulatory narratives.
