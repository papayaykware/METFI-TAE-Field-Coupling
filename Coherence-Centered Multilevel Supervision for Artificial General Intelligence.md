# Abstract

This paper proposes a coherence-centered architecture for Artificial General Intelligence based on the Theory of Learning by Exception (TAE). The model is structured around three interdependent supervisory modules: logical (cortical), axiological (cardiac), and operational (enteric). Stability is defined not as logical consistency alone, but as dynamic synchronization across representational, value-based, and execution layers.

The framework introduces error-profile coupling, toroidal feedback topology, nonlinear stability constraints, and exception-triggered structural reorganization. Mathematical formalization and experimental monitoring programs are presented to support reproducibility and further development.

# 1. Introduction

AGI design traditionally focuses on generalization capacity and alignment mechanisms. However, these dimensions are often treated independently. This separation is structurally unstable.

In complex adaptive systems, collapse rarely results from isolated failure. It emerges from desynchronization across regulatory layers.

TAE reframes learning as structural reorganization triggered by exceptional incoherence. Within this perspective, AGI stability requires distributed supervision across differentiated domains.
